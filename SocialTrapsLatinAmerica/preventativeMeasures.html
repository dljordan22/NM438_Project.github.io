Preventative Measures and Public Response


Latin America has been a land of resistance to all forms of oppression since the arrival of westerners. These problems often correlate with the spread of capitalism and neoliberalism, and largely work at the expense of indigenous, impoverished, and non-white individuals. Reform programs and Resistance movements are often spearheaded by these same groups, working to end basic governmental negligence and the oligarchies in which they are born under.



- High increase within the past 30 years
- International organizations created to work against these forms of corruption
- Enhanced public transparency via audits, disclosure agreements, more forms of government accountability, and less government discretion
- The ways in which the web/social media play a part in public information and resistance
- How much news and media outlets are allowed to cover on these matters
- Urgency of economic downturn
- Heightened focus on institutional transformation and what is “normal”
- Pro-equity policy
- Public access to technology
